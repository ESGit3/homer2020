Notable People

Tiresias

A Theban Prophet, often seen with his golden scepter. Tiresias is well known for providing advice to some of the most important people in history, including Odysseus and Oedipus. If you are lucky enough, you may be able to see his shade around the House of Death, advising those who have significant journeys up ahead.

Tyro

The daughter of Salmoneus and the wife of Crethus. She was the mother of Aeson, Pheres and Amythaon, as well as the more famous twins Pelias and Neleus.

Antiope

The daughter of Asopus, who is "proud she’d spent a night in the arms of Zeus himself and borne the god twin sons, Amphion and Zethus, the first to build the footings of seven-gated Thebes" (182).

Epicaste

The mother of Oedipus, who in all innocence, abandoned her son, who later went on to kill his father and marry his mother. Many criticize her for what she did and many defend her actions.

Achilles

A hero of the Trojan War, and a man who the "Argives honored as a god" (190). Achilles is known for his bravery and strength, especially during the Trojan war. He was dunked in the River Styx by his mother in the hopes of making him invincible, with just his heel keeping him somewhat mortal. He died by an arrow hitting his heel, his only vulnerable spot, hence the term "Achilles Heel".

Sisyphus

A king of Ephyra, known for his craftiness and deceitfulness. He cheated death two times, and thus ended up with the punishment of pushing a boulder up a mountain every day, just to have it roll back to the bottom, forcing him to restart. He is often seen with "sweat drenching his body, dust swirling above his head" (194).

Minos

The first king of Crete, and a judge of the dead, determining the fate of others in the underworld. You will see large crowds of people pleading for his verdicts. Not only can you meet him through our tours, you will also meet him when you will eventually be judged in the afterlife.

Agamemnon

Agamemnon was a famous field marshal who died at the hands of his brother, Aegisthus, as well as his wife. His shade is quite noticable, as Odysseus described him as "fraught with grief and flanked by all his comrades, troops of his men-at-arms who died beside him, who met their fate in lord Aegisthus’ halls" (187). If you notice a large legion of soldiers around the underworld, there is a chance that Agamemnon may be there.

Tantalus

A wicked king of Sipylus. He is most well known for the punishment that he received. Visitors to the Underworld will be able to see Tantalus suspended in the middle of a lake with fruits above him, but he will never be able to drink or eat as they just rise or fall out of reach. This unique punishment has been an attraction for many, and is a must-see for visitors.
